Polyparent

Polyparent

Brief / Speculative design to provoke discussion
Agency / Avans University Breda
My role / Concept designer
Team / Bram, Freek

Imagine you and your partner working full time and deadlines force you to bring unfinished work home. Both of you want a child, but how on earth will you be able to fit a child into your busy life? Part time parenting could be the answer.

Polyparent is a service that matches you to others who want a child, based on your income and availability. When people combined have enough time and money to take good care of a child, a match is made and the child will be created in a lab. Combining DNA will assure that everyone involved is truly the biological parent from the child.

When a child has more parents, the burdens will be less. Therefore, sharing a child is not only convenient for the parents but also for the child. The child receives the comprehensive care and attention that he or she deserves. The solution to combine your packed work schedules with parenthood. But what are the consequences?

Design process

Topic
We tried creative ways of brainstorming to find an interesting topic, like throwing a marker as a dart towards a list of random words. With these words we thought of statements that could start a discussion regarding a current social trend.

New approach
Through research we found a map full of upcoming trends in technology and the words ‘three-parent-pregnancy’ caught our eyes. Our interest grew and we looked deeper into it.


Research
The topic started a lot of discussion and raised many questions, which was good. Through research we amplified how realistic this idea was and what kind of influence this world would have on different contexts.

Speculative Design
We came up with the statement: ‘what if we could have children with more than two biological parents?’ to create discussion. Hilarious images of multiple parents raising a child came to mind, and we started working on a visualization. Eventually we designed Alana, a stroller for shared parenthood.
